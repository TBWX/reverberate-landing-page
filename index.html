Excerpt from Reverberate: The Blame We Exchange

"We weren't just fighting each other—we were fighting ourselves."

He looked at me like I was the problem. And maybe I was. But not in the way he made it seem.

One night, after yet another argument that spiraled into silence, I found myself staring at him while he scrolled through his phone—again—and I realized: he wasn't going to stop. Not lying. Not deflecting. Not pretending I hadn't just asked him a direct question.

I wasn't just angry at him. I was angry at myself—for needing an answer I already knew, for hoping this time would be different, and for calling it love when it was really just recognition... the familiarity of dysfunction.

The worst part? I still wanted him to hold me.

That's what this book is about. That quiet pull to stay even when the truth is screaming. The echoes we ignore until they become patterns. The way we lie to ourselves and call it forgiveness.

I thought I could fix it.

I thought if I explained myself better, loved harder, gave more, I'd finally be enough to make him choose me over the silence.

But I was never in competition with another person.

I was in competition with the version of himself he was afraid to confront.

And the version of myself I refused to let go of.

Sometimes, the mirror doesn't break. It just reflects the parts of us we're not brave enough to face. Yet.
